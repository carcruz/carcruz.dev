[Pounce](https://colabobio.medium.com/become-a-community-scientist-by-watching-puppy-videos-with-pounce-314c6b32f0a0) is a citizen science platform built with the [Colubri Lab](https://www.colubrilab.org/) that lets volunteers contribute to animal behavior research by watching short video clips and tagging specific actions. The initial focus is identifying "grab-bite" behaviors in puppies — a task that would otherwise require hours of expert review per video.

By crowdsourcing the annotation process, the platform dramatically speeds up dataset generation and opens the door to larger, more complex behavioral studies. The resulting data is also a foundation for training AI models for automated animal behavior analysis.

The project was developed in collaboration with Elinor Karlsson's Lab and launched publicly at MIT Museum After Dark in August 2025.

### Role

**Frontend Lead & Architect** — led the frontend development and architected the web application. Collaborated with the Colubri Lab engineering team (Sanskruti Magdum, Jacob Collins, Mansi Khandpekar) on the full platform.

### Technical Architecture

- **Frontend**: Web-based video tagging interface designed for ease of use with non-expert volunteers — architecture and implementation led by me
- **Backend**: Cloud infrastructure for storing and processing volunteer annotations at scale
